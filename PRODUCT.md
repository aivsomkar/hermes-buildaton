# LaunchReel product context

register: brand

## Product purpose
LaunchReel is an AI launch-video agency for software companies. A customer provides their product URL and an inspiration video. A Hermes director coordinates specialists that study the product, deconstruct the reference, write the story, and render a 30–60 second launch video using the customer’s real product UI.

## Users
The primary users are startup founders and small software teams preparing a product or feature launch. They are short on time, cannot justify a traditional agency, and still care deeply about tasteful motion, accurate product representation, and a deliverable they can publish immediately.

## Core promise
Paste the product. Show us the taste. Get a launch video that feels made for this product, not generated from a generic template.

## Strategic principles
1. The customer’s real UI is the hero. Stock footage is garnish, never the product.
2. Inspiration transfers structure and motion grammar, not copyrighted media.
3. Show the agent crew doing real work. Progress, handoffs, and outputs should be legible.
4. A useful draft must survive provider failure. The local fallback still produces a script, style brief, and playable MP4.
5. The interface should feel like a creative production desk, not another AI chat box.

## Brand voice
Decisive, cinematic, and technically literate. Short sentences. Concrete verbs. No hype fog. The brand behaves like a sharp creative director who also understands render pipelines.

## Anti-references
Generic purple SaaS gradients, glass dashboards, glowing AI orbs, fake chat-first interfaces, identical feature-card grids, and stock footage pretending to be a software product.

## Success state
Within one screen the user understands the two-input model, can submit a job, and can watch the crew progress from research through analysis, scripting, and render. The final state exposes the style brief, script, and MP4.
